Day 3

Thursday 25 June 20268 talks

Day 3 focused on the practical application of simulation-based inference (SBI) and machine learning to bridge the gap between complex galaxy evolution models and large-scale observational data. The sessions spanned a wide range of scales, from the internal ionization regions of individual galaxies and the Epoch of Reionization to the large-scale structure of the cosmic web. A recurring emphasis was placed on improving the robustness of these inferences, specifically through the use of multifidelity simulations, contrastive learning, and methods to mitigate model misspecification.

Day 2

Wednesday 24 June 202615 talks

The second day of SBI4GALEV focused on the practical implementation and scaling of simulation-based inference across diverse astrophysical domains, from galaxy clustering and AGN spectra to gravitational waves. A significant portion of the day was dedicated to overcoming the 'simulation bottleneck' through emulators, multi-fidelity models, and generative AI. The sessions highlighted a transition toward more flexible, amortized inference tools—particularly diffusion models—that can handle missing data, non-Gaussian noise, and evolving priors. The day concluded with a call for community standardization through a proposed SBI data challenge for galaxy evolution.

Day 1

Tuesday 23 June 202615 talks

The first day of SBI4GALEV focused on the transition from traditional likelihood-based methods to Simulation-Based Inference (SBI) and Likelihood-Free Inference (LFI) in galaxy evolution. Presentations spanned the development of high-performance differentiable frameworks in JAX, the use of generative models and emulators to bypass expensive radiative transfer and N-body simulations, and the application of neural networks to disentangle complex physical degeneracies in SEDs and kinematics. The day established a clear trajectory toward amortized inference, where machine learning is used to provide fast, scalable, and uncertainty-aware parameter estimation for large-scale astronomical surveys.
