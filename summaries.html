Day 3

Day 3 of SBI4GALEV focused on the practical application of simulation-based inference (SBI) to bridge the gap between complex astrophysical simulations and observational data. The sessions spanned a wide range of scales, from the internal physics of the interstellar medium and nebular structures to large-scale cosmological surveys and weak lensing fields. A recurring theme was the optimization of the inference process, specifically through techniques to reduce computational costs, handle model misspecification, and leverage multi-fidelity data. Together, these talks demonstrated how SBI is evolving from a theoretical tool into a robust framework for enhancing the precision of galaxy evolution and cosmological constraints.

Day 2

Wednesday 24 June 202611 talks

The second day of SBI4GALEV focused on the practical implementation and scaling of Simulation-Based Inference across diverse astrophysical applications, from galaxy clustering and SED fitting to gravitational waves. A significant portion of the day was dedicated to overcoming the 'simulation bottleneck' through emulators, multi-fidelity models, and generative AI, such as diffusion models and flow-matching. The sessions bridged the gap between theoretical framework development—including robust loss functions and prior adaptation—and community-driven efforts to benchmark these tools against traditional Bayesian methods.

Day 1

Tuesday 23 June 20268 talks

The first day of SBI4GALEV focused on the integration of Simulation-Based Inference (SBI) and machine learning to overcome the computational bottlenecks of traditional galaxy evolution modeling. Presentations spanned the development of fast neural emulators for spectral energy distributions (SEDs) and radiative transfer, the use of diffusion models for population synthesis and kinematics, and the creation of differentiable frameworks for GPU optimization. The sessions collectively highlighted a shift toward amortized inference and generative modeling to handle complex, high-dimensional astronomical data more efficiently than classical MCMC or nested sampling methods.
